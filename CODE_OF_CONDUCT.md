# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

In the interest of fostering an open, professional, and welcoming community, maintainers and contributors of Polar.DB are committed to making participation respectful and constructive for everyone.

We want this project to be a place where people can discuss code, design, bugs, performance, documentation, and architecture without harassment, hostility, or needless gatekeeping.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment include:

- being respectful in discussions and reviews;
- focusing on technical substance rather than personal attacks;
- accepting good-faith criticism;
- giving clear, actionable feedback;
- acknowledging uncertainty where it exists;
- being careful with claims about correctness, safety, and performance.

## Unacceptable behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- harassment, insults, or intimidation;
- discriminatory or demeaning language;
- trolling, flamebait, or deliberately hostile review behavior;
- publishing private information without permission;
- repeatedly derailing technical discussion;
- knowingly misrepresenting benchmarks, correctness, or security impact.

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for setting the tone of the project and for enforcing this Code of Conduct fairly and consistently.

They may remove, edit, or reject comments, issues, pull requests, code, or other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in project spaces, including:

- issues;
- pull requests;
- code review discussions;
- discussions around documentation, releases, and support.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it privately to the maintainers through a private contact channel associated with the repository owner or project maintainers.

Security-sensitive reports should follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate, including:

- asking for a change in behavior;
- deleting comments;
- locking conversations;
- closing issues or pull requests;
- temporarily or permanently restricting participation.
